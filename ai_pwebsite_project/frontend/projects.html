MultiView Synchro (Class Project - Human-Computer Interaction)

Purpose:

Designed a prototype alternative to Synchro Studio that improved usability through enhanced tool discoverability and customizable workspace.

Motivation:

Our team of five identified two pain points in Synchro Studio's rigid interface, making it difficult for new and experienced users to efficiently locate tools and manage their workspace.

Key Highlights:

  • Conducted contextual inquiry and user interviews to uncover usability issues.
  • Developed iterative prototypes, from sketches and paper mockups to high-fidelity Figma designs.
  • Introduced a global search bar and flexible panel system for improved user control.

What I Learned:

I strengthened my UX design skills by applying research methods, building interactive protoypes, and integrating feedback. Throughout this project, I also gained valuable experience in group communication, role coordination, and collaborative problem-solving.

Drones are Threatening Your Privacy and Security (Computer Ethics Paper)

Purpose:

This paper analyzes how the rise of unmanned aerial vehicles (drones) introduce privacy risks while proposing ethical and policy-driven solutions.

Motivation:

I wanted to explore how rapid technological innovation can outpace laws and regulations, using drones as a case study of both convenience and risk in society.

Key Highlights:

  • Discusses surveillance risks and unauthorized data collection from drones.
  • Examines existing policy and regulatory gaps in current U.S law that permits such risks.
  • Proposes redefined ethical frameworks and realistic solutions for policymakers.

What I Learned:

I strengthened my technical writing skills in developing this paper for an academic audience and my research methods in connecting technical issues to ethical standards.
